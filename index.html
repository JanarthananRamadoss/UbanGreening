WHAT TO DO URBAN GREENING

Why planting trees?

Plants absorb pollutants and release oxygen, purifying the air. Increasing the number of trees in urban areas helps improve air quality, provide shade, and reduce the urban heat island effect .
Planting trees in urban areas is a crucial aspect of urban greening and comes with several benefits:

1.AIR QUALITY IMPROVEMENT: Trees absorb pollutants such as carbon dioxide, sulfur dioxide, and ozone, thereby improving air quality.

2.COOLING EFFECT: Trees provide shade and reduce the urban heat island effect, lowering temperatures in cities and decreasing energy use for cooling.

3.STORMWATER MANAGEMENT: Tree roots help absorb rainwater, reducing runoff and the risk of flooding.

4.WILDLIFE HABITAT: Trees provide habitats for birds, insects, and other wildlife, contributing to urban biodiversity.

5.AESTHETIC ENHANCEMENT: Trees beautify urban landscapes, making cities more pleasant and attractive places to live and work.

6.HEALTH BENIFITS: Access to green spaces and trees has been linked to improved mental health and reduced stress levels among urban residents.

When planting trees in urban areas, it's essential to consider factors such as species selection (choosing native species adapted to local conditions), location (planting in areas where trees can thrive and provide maximum benefits), and maintenance (watering, pruning, and protecting young trees from damage).

Community involvement and engagement in tree-planting initiatives can also foster a sense of ownership and responsibility, ensuring the long-term success and sustainability of urban greening efforts.
                      

Why rooftop gardens are be choose?

Rooftop gardens reduce urban heat island effect and provide green spaces. certainly! Rooftop gardens are an excellent choice for urban areas due to several compelling reasons. First, they maximize space by transforming otherwise unused rooftops into lush green areas, even in densely populated cities. Second, rooftop gardens enhance energy efficiency by acting as natural insulators, reducing the urban heat island effect. Third, they contribute to cleaner air by capturing pollutants and releasing oxygen. Additionally, these green spaces help manage stormwater runoff, provide habitats for wildlife, and create tranquil sanctuaries amidst the hustle and bustle of city life.
